Week 5


5 Levels (July 16 — July 20)

The report structure is clearer and we designed a 5-level system to represent the degree of complexity and completeness of the report. Level 1 and level 5 are easy because they are pretty clear-cut, but levels 2-4 are still fuzzy. I wish we could finish this part as soon as possible so we can work on other stuff.

It was very interesting to attend the reading group on Thursday so I get to know a little bit more about this field. We talked about various things about the integration of technology in the life of disabled people. Anat told us the story of a nonvocal girl’s friend seeing the girl’s always accompanied assistant as a friend, too. She notified the importance of respecting the fact that human is part of the process of helping the handicapped. I never thought about that and it was definitely food for thought.

Week 3


Report Ideas (July 2 — July 6)

I focused on gathering ideas on the report this week. Basically, the report on an individual app would demonstrate the most necessary features of an app to a user from the perspective of accessibility. We are planning on divide the report into two big parts, one for VoiceOver/TalkBack, and one for Access Control/Switch Scanning. The hardest part is separating the users’ needs from more technical stuff. I am constantly wavering between the user perspective and programming perspective.

On Sunday, I went to the first ever Amazon GO in Seattle. It was absolutely worth it. Amazon GO is a new style of convenience store where the user can swipe in with an app and buy anything they want without checking out. The app keeps track of the shopping list in a virtual cart, and the user can receive a receipt shortly after. The whole experience was great. I was so excited see this kind of technology. The only downside is that the store is relatively small. I wish Amazon would consider to build bigger grocery store with the same technology.

Week 1


Planning and Preparing (June 18 — June 22)

The first week finally came! The main thing I did is to understand the project and to start preparing and plan for the whole program. After a discussion with Anat and reading a paper from a past student project, I began to understand our goals. To start, I needed a questionnaire to ask specific questions for the assessment. I adapted the Google form for Android app assessment and replaced some of the features with the equivalent in the iOS system. Later, we could use the result to compare accessibility features between iOS apps and Android apps and probably find out if there is any difference between open sourced and proprietary developing environment.

Seattle is a beautiful city. I lived in an apartment close to the university. The apartment itself is neat and my roommate seems nice, the only thing that bothers me is that street not far away looks sketch and some college students next door party almost everyday. I’m planning to travel a lot on weekends when I’m still in Seattle, but it is a bit hard when I don’t have a car.
