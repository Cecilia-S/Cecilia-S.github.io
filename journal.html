Week 10

August 20 — August 24


The End!!!

I can’t believe this is the last week! We finalized the 5 stages (previously 5 levels) in the report following a clinical model. The report template was adjusted based on the stages and most charts in the spreadsheet are ready to go. I tried to make a sample report using Emma’s data, but the end result shows that there’s still issues waiting to be solved. Here are the things need to be worked on in the future: 1) Emma’s assessment provides some inaccurate answers that are most likely caused by misunderstanding the question. 2) Some charts in the report are not well presented. 3) The forms should have additional questions. 4) We need to assign an unique id to each user. 5) Some concepts are not well-defined. 6) More data need to be generated.

Anyway, Anat and I had lunch together and discussed some future plans. I will keep working on this project. The summer research itself was fun and I learned a lot about research in general and myself as a person. Hopefully I’ll use of the skills I learned later in my research career.

5 Levels

The report structure is clearer and we designed a 5-level system to represent the degree of complexity and completeness of the report. Level 1 and level 5 are easy because they are pretty clear-cut, but levels 2-4 are still fuzzy. I wish we could finish this part as soon as possible so we can work on other stuff.

It was very interesting to attend the reading group on Thursday so I get to know a little bit more about this field. We talked about various things about the integration of technology in the life of disabled people. Anat told us the story of a nonvocal girl’s friend seeing the girl’s always accompanied assistant as a friend, too. Week 3

July 2 — July 6


Report Ideas

I focused on gathering ideas on the report this week. Basically, the report on an individual app would demonstrate the most necessary features of an app to a user from the perspective of accessibility. We are planning on divide the report into two big parts, one for VoiceOver/TalkBack, and one for Access Control/Switch Scanning. The hardest part is separating the users’ needs from more technical stuff. I am constantly wavering between the user perspective and programming perspective.

On Sunday, I went to the first ever Amazon GO in Seattle. It was absolutely worth it. Amazon GO is a new style of convenience store where the user can swipe in with an app and buy anything they want without checking out. The app keeps track of the shopping list in a virtual cart, and the user can receive a receipt shortly after. The whole experience was great. I was so excited see this kind of technology. The only downside is that the store is relatively small. I wish Amazon would consider to build bigger grocery store with the same technology.

Planning and Preparing

The first week finally came! The main thing I did is to understand the project and to start preparing and plan for the whole program.
