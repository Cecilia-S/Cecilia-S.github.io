Mobile Accessibility Assessment Project

Mobile accessibility concerns the effort of making mobile devices accessible to people with disabilities. Despite the various guidelines that address common problems and list out essential components to create accessible technology, software developers often dismiss important accessibility features due to various reasons, including ignorance and misunderstanding. Moreover, existing assessment tools for web and application accessibility could only check for missing information and page layout without reflecting how the well the application could perform the core functions using accessibility services.

The focus of the proposed research is to develop an indicator that could accurately reflect the level of accessibility of an application and help users, especially users with disabilities, to better evaluate the functionality of the app. To achieve the goal, we assess 35 applications developed in the iOS environment to look for prevalent accessibility barriers. We also compare the accessibility of iOS apps with earlier assessments of Android apps to collect differences between open source and proprietary applications.

The project will be divided into the following main components.

iOS application assessment

This part of the research is to assess the accessibilities of 35 iOS applications in order to gain insights of the common problems in existing apps. The assessment will be performed by hand. By completing detailed questionnaires, a student will evaluate the accessibilities of two functions for each application. Using different iOS accessibility services, the student will perform tasks on an iPhone or an emulator and provide detailed answers to describe how accessible the application is.

Compare app accessibility between Android apps and iOS apps

On the basis of earlier projects on Android application accessibility, this project focuses on assessments of iOS applications and provides the opportunity to compare Android applications with iOS ones. The comparison could present the differences between open source and proprietary applications in the respect of accessibility.

Accessibility guidelines

This part of the research focuses on how to present the level of app accessibility in a way that an user could easily understand.

“Accessibility is all about removing barriers and providing the benefits of technology for everyone.”
